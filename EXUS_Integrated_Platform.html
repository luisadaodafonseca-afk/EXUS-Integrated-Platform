Industrial Solutions

The Orchestration Engine: Four Supply Layers

Industrial Solutions fills the full 24/7 demand profile by aggregating four supply layers into one shaped, bankable energy product.
01

Behind-the-Meter (BTM)

Co-located energy assets at site — solar, wind, BESS, gas peakers. Lowest cost, full price certainty. This is the Energy Island foundation.

02

Exus IPP Portfolio

4.1 GW of owned operating assets connected through the grid via structured PPAs — direct supply capacity from Exus Renewables.

03

Third-Party Managed Assets

3.3 GW surplus capacity from Exus-managed external portfolios — additional supply optionality and hedging capability.

04

Market Contracts & Real-Time Trading

Residual shaping via standard PPAs, spot markets, grid integration — real-time optimisation fills remaining load gaps.

Industrial Solutions controls the aggregation logic across all four layers. The PPA is the commercial expression of the technical system — Exus does not sell electrons; it designs bankable energy ecosystems translated into contractual structures.

Platform Value Creation

The Self-Reinforcing Flywheel

ER + ED creates four structural dependencies that would not exist in a standalone configuration.
01

Balance Sheet Support

ED's competitive positioning begins with credible grid bonds. The EUR 21.1M bond for Setubal was underwritten by ER's contracted revenue base. Less than EUR 5M of ~EUR 30M deployed is drawn cash equity.

EUR 21.1M grid bond
02

Energy Cost Advantage

The 40–60% energy cost reduction is the direct output of Exus's proprietary TCO modelling and owned generation. For a 1 GW campus, this saves over €1bn/year vs. standard grid procurement.

~58% cost reduction
03

Capital Recycling

ER's develop-to-rotate model funds ED's DevEx. BESS established the develop-to-exit precedent; Skyward is the next rotation. Without ER, ED requires permanent external equity at higher cost.

Self-financing engine
04

Shock Absorption

ED carries binary monetisation risk. ER's 4.1 GW asset base absorbs project-level failures. A standalone ED entity would be existentially exposed to a single early-stage failure.

4.1 GW asset base

ER does not exist to supply ED. The portfolios were built on their own merits, for their own clients. What makes the combination valuable is the platform layer: balance sheet, TSO access, local teams, energy structuring capability, and capital recycling.

Strategic Implications

Three Imperatives for the Board

The integrated platform thesis is validated by execution and by the market. These are the priorities.
1

Protect the Integration

The ER + ED platform synergy is the core competitive moat. Any structural separation of the energy-digital interface would eliminate the very advantages that position Exus uniquely. Industrial Solutions must remain the central orchestrator connecting development, commercial, and operational capabilities.

2

Scale the Energy Island Model

The Hohe Börde framework should become the standard playbook across all ED pipeline sites. Each new campus should be developed with its full BTM energy architecture from day one. The Phase 3 pipeline (demand-aligned development) embeds this logic already — accelerate it.

3

Communicate the Thesis Externally

The powered land asset class is attracting massive institutional capital. Exus's differentiation — integrated energy solution, not just grid access — must be clearly articulated to hyperscaler clients, investment partners, and capital allocators. The competitive frameworks in this document provide the positioning architecture.

The integrated model is not a strategy choice — it is the strategy. ED is an extension of ER into a segment where the platform's existing capabilities create exceptional value. Very few are doing this globally. In Europe, only Exus.
